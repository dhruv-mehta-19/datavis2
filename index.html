The Heart of Coffee

Why Melbourne earned the title — from bean to cup

By Dhruv Mehta · FIT2179 Data Visualisation 2 · May 2026

Melbourne isn't the city where Australians drink the most coffee — that title belongs to Western Australia by some measures, or Canberra by others. So why is Melbourne famous for it? This is the story of how a city without the highest consumption built a culture that the world calls the coffee capital.

01The World That Supplies It

Before it lands in a Carlton café, every bean travels through Australia's import network. In 2023, Australia imported coffee from over 50 countries — but a handful do almost all the work. Brazil alone supplies nearly twice what Colombia does, our second biggest supplier.

Ranked by value, the top suppliers tell a clear story: Latin America dominates Australian imports, with South-East Asia (Vietnam, Indonesia) and Africa (Ethiopia, Kenya) playing supporting roles.

02Premium Beans vs Bulk Beans

Not every bean is created equal. Vietnam supplies us with cheap robusta at $2.58 per kilogram — by far the lowest price of any major partner. At the other end, Jamaica Blue Mountain commands $62 per kilogram in tiny boutique quantities. Australia imports both — the workhorses that fill chain-store espressos, and the luxury beans that end up in specialty cafés.

03The City That Drinks It

The City of Melbourne alone hosts around 3,000 cafés and restaurants, packed into a few square kilometres. The CBD dominates, but Carlton's Lygon Street has been the cultural heart of Melbourne's coffee scene since Italian and Greek migrants brought espresso here in the 1950s.

Looking at the numbers another way: Melbourne CBD has more cafés than every other neighbourhood combined.

04Where Your $5 Goes

So why does a flat white cost what it does? Most of your money isn't going to the beans or the milk — it's going to the barista, the rent, and the GST. Even before paying any overheads, the café only nets $2.22 from a $5 cup. And in 2024–25, most Melbourne cafés have pushed prices to $6 just to keep the lights on.

05What Makes Melbourne Coffee Special

It's not just the cafés — it's the network behind every cup. Fresh roasting from local independents like Market Lane and Padre. Soft Melbourne water that extracts beans differently from Sydney's harder supply. A post-war migration history that brought espresso culture here in the 1950s. Baristas treated as professionals, not casuals. The result is a city where mediocrity gets driven out of business by the café two doors down.
